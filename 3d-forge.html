3D Forge — How to Use

Core Features

  • Load Models: Click library samples, drag & drop .glb/.gltf files, or paste public URLs.
  • Camera: Left mouse drag to rotate • Scroll to zoom • Right drag to pan. Excellent OrbitControls with damping.
  • Materials & Skins: After loading, expand Materials section → upload new albedo/roughness/normal maps for live updates.
  • Animations: If present in the GLB, they appear in the Animations panel. Play, scrub, adjust speed.
  • AI Skin Prompts: Go to the "AI Skin Prompts" tab. Fill the form based on your model. Generate a highly optimized prompt for Grok Imagine or any AI. Copy → generate image → upload as texture.

Pro Tips

  • GLB is best (everything embedded). glTF works if all files are together (harder with uploads).
  • For your own large library: Download this HTML, put it + models folder on a local server (python -m http.server). Add relative paths to the samples list in code.
  • Performance: Complex models with many textures may be heavy. Use wireframe or lower res for testing.
  • The prompt tool is tuned for PBR game assets and works amazingly with Grok Imagine.
