THE VISION

Beyond individual agents.
Toward genuine multi-scale intelligence.

Most artificial life projects focus on evolving better individual creatures. This project takes a fundamentally different approach: it explores what happens when complexity is allowed to stack across multiple layers of organization.

The goal is not just more sophisticated agents, but the emergence of higher-order entities — structures that contain other complex structures, with their own identity, goals, and internal dynamics.

This is the path toward what we’re calling Thronglet-level emergence: recursive, multi-scale beings capable of genuine collective agency, culture, and open-ended evolution.

HOW IT WORKS

Core Architecture

Creatures & Modules

Every agent carries a set of evolvable modules (harvester, sensor, armor, storage, mover, efficient) that directly influence both its physical capabilities and its neural architecture. Modules can be damaged, upgraded through evolution, and passed to offspring with variation.

This creates strong selection pressure for both physical form and cognitive specialization.

MacroOrganisms

When enough creatures with compatible roles stay close together over time, they can form MacroOrganisms — higher-level entities that absorb members, develop permanent upgrades through module condensation, and exhibit emergent specialization (Forager macros, Scout macros, Sensor macros, etc.).

Internal Ecosystems

Macros contain lightweight InternalAgents with roles, energy economies, reproduction, and conflict. This creates a second layer of evolution happening inside the macro — a foundation for distributed cognition and internal resource politics.

Sonic Communication

Creatures emit procedurally generated signals influenced by their emotional state, energy level, recent success, and role. These signals create choruses, rhythmic calls, alliance songs, and ghost echoes that influence group behavior and coordination.

NEURAL SYSTEMS

How the Brains Work

Each creature has an evolvable neural network with several advanced features that go well beyond standard reinforcement learning setups:

Structural Evolution: The network can grow new neurons and prune weak connections across generations. This is guided by both random mutation and lifetime importance tracking (weight magnitude × activation impact).
Parallel Pathways: The network maintains two slightly different computation paths (Module_A and Module_B) that can be dynamically weighted based on role, context (desperate vs safe), and recent success.
Recurrent Memory: Creatures maintain a hidden recurrent state across time steps using a lightweight GRU-style mechanism. This allows them to integrate information over multiple frames rather than reacting only to the current moment.
Module Influence: The physical modules a creature carries directly influence how its neural network develops and which pathways it favors. This creates tight coupling between body and mind.
Heritable Emotional Sensitivities: Creatures inherit and mutate sensitivities to fear, loneliness, contentment, curiosity, and aggression. These affect signaling, decision-making, and rhythmic vocalization style.

Important: This is not a standard reinforcement learning setup. The networks evolve across generations through mutation and selection, with some lifetime learning signals. The goal is open-ended structural and behavioral diversity rather than optimization toward a single task.

EMERGENT BEHAVIOR

What Actually Emerges

Role Specialization

Creatures naturally differentiate into Foragers, Scouts, Couriers, and Sensors through the interaction of modules, neural structure, and environmental pressures. These roles are not hardcoded — they emerge.

Macro-Level Personality

Macros develop stable specializations (e.g. aggressive expansion vs careful resource hoarding) based on which modules get absorbed most often and which members succeed over time.

Communication Protocols

Rhythmic signaling and chorus behavior emerge spontaneously when groups of creatures with compatible states are near each other. These can evolve into alliance-level “songs” and rituals.

Internal Resource Politics

Inside macros, different internal roles compete and cooperate for limited nutrients, creating simple internal economies and selection pressures at a second scale.

Persistent Group Identity

Highly stable, well-connected macros begin to develop consistent home ranges, strategic preferences, and long-term behavioral biases — early signs of emergent group-level goals.
