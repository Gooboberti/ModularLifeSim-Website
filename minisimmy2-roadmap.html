1. Philosophy & Goals

MiniSimmy2 is an agent-based artificial life simulation focused on emergence rather than traditional gameplay loops.

The core design goal is to create a system where interesting, sometimes surprising behavior arises naturally from the interaction of relatively simple rules over long periods of time.

Emergence First
Interesting behavior should come from systems interacting, not from hand-crafted scripts.
Long-term Investment
The most meaningful progression comes from sculpting lineages across many generations through the Gene Vault.
Player as Steward
The player influences the ecosystem rather than directly controlling individual creatures.

2. Core Simulation Loop

The simulation runs on a standard p5.js draw() loop at ~60fps. Every frame, the following major systems are updated in order:

  1. 1. Environmental cycle progress (Fertile → Normal → Drought)
  2. 2. All creatures update their position, energy, behavior, and thoughts
  3. 3. Vortex particle movement and attraction
  4. 4. Hazard updates (Red particles, Energy Walls, Cursor Hazard)
  5. 5. Reproduction checks and new creature spawning
  6. 6. Death checks and energy orb release
  7. 7. UI updates (stats, inspector, furnace timer, etc.)
  8. 8. Auto Alpha tracking (if enabled)

3. Creature Architecture

Every creature is an instance of the Creature class. They are relatively lightweight objects that carry their own state and behavior logic.

Core Properties

  • Position & Velocity — Standard 2D movement with friction
  • Energy — Primary resource. Dies at 0
  • Modules — Array of module objects (the "brain")
  • Generation — How many generations removed from original
  • Specialization — Dominant module type
  • Traits — Four personality values (pheromone sensitivity, exploration bias, vortex resistance, hazard avoidance)
  • isPredator — Boolean state
  • currentThought — Short string shown above creature

Key Methods

  • update() — Main behavior loop every frame
  • show() — Rendering
  • reproduce() — Creates offspring with inherited modules + mutation
  • updateSpecialization() — Determines role based on modules
  • checkForTierUpgrades() — Merges 3 identical modules into next tier
  • pruneExcessModules() — Keeps module count reasonable

4. Modules — The Creature Brain

Modules are the fundamental unit of intelligence in MiniSimmy2. They are not traditional neural networks — they are more like specialized organs or instincts that bias behavior in specific ways.

Module Color Core Behavior Influence Tier V Effect
Harvester Increases energy gained from food zones. Better at collecting white particles. Significantly larger pickup radius
Sensor Improves hazard detection range and quality. Enables predictive avoidance. Can dodge from much farther away
Mover Helps creatures escape the vortex center more effectively. Very strong spiral escape ability
Resistant Reduces base energy drain rate. Extremely strong in Drought phases
Explorer Increases random movement and exploration bias. Helps break out of movement loops
Communicator Strengthens pheromone signals and hazard warnings to nearby creatures. Very effective group coordination
Tier Merging Rule

When a creature accumulates 3 modules of the same type at the same tier, they automatically merge into one module of the next tier. This is checked periodically in checkForTierUpgrades().

5. How Creatures Think & Behave

Creatures do not have a central "brain" object. Instead, their behavior emerges from a combination of:

  • Module counts and tiers
  • Current energy level
  • Environmental state (day/night, phase)
  • Proximity to hazards and other creatures
  • Internal trait values
  • Current isPredator state

Behavior Priority Order (Simplified)

1. Hazard Avoidance — Strongest immediate response if Sensors detect danger.
2. Energy Seeking — Move toward active food zone.
3. Social Alignment — Match velocity and position with similar neighbors.
4. Vortex Forces — Constant pull + rotation from the center.
5. Reproduction — If energy is high and cooldown is ready.
6. Predator Hunting — Only active when isPredator is true.

6. Environmental Systems

Fertile Season
High energy gain • High reproduction • Easier survival
Normal Conditions
Balanced values. Standard difficulty.
Drought
High energy drain • Low reproduction • Harsh conditions. Rewards Resistant + Explorer creatures.

7. Hazards & Player Tools

Red Hazard Particles

Basic damaging particles. Can be toggled in the Cheats store. Strong selective pressure that rewards Sensor modules.

Cursor Hazard

Turns the mouse into a damaging field. Also absorbs nearby red particles when active. Very strong tool for direct intervention.

Energy Walls (v1.95+)

Player-placed barriers. Come in two main types:

  • Solid — Affects all creatures. Expensive to draw.
  • Selective Filter — Only affects specific types (Predators, High Module, Low Module, White/Red particles). Unaffected creatures pass through completely.

8. Economy & Progression

Progression in MiniSimmy2 happens on two main tracks:

  • Short-term: High Roller Points, Furnace multiplier, temporary power-ups.
  • Long-term: Aetherium Crystals, Gene Vault, preserved high-quality brains across resets.

9. Gene Vault (Current Major Feature)

The Gene Vault is the primary long-term progression system. It allows players to preserve high-quality neural patterns (brains) across simulation resets.

Core Concept: Instead of prestiging just for points, players can now invest in lineage quality. Brains stored in the vault influence future generations through special prestige particles.

10. Creature Inspector & Neural Visualization

The inspector provides deep visibility into individual creatures, including a live neural net visualization showing inputs, hidden layer, and outputs with activity pulsing.

11. Emergent Behavior

Some observed emergent behaviors include flocking by module type, internal group competition leading to predator conversion, strong Sensor creatures becoming extremely good at hazard avoidance, and Explorer-heavy populations performing better during Drought phases.
