Detailed Systems Explanation

Yellow Honey Blooms (Food Blooms)
Deposited by living creatures when they have excess stored honey (especially Harvesters). They travel toward the currently active food node (green during day, blue at night). They are affected by the vortex — most reach the main node, but some get caught by the spin and eventually freeze into secondary static food patches. These frozen patches emit attraction pheromones and can be eaten from directly.
Green Energy Orbs (Traveling)
These are the green orbs you see traveling in straight lines toward the active food nodes alongside the yellow honey. They are released primarily when creatures die (energy recycling) and sometimes on other events. They head directly to the currently active zone to return energy to the ecosystem. They are distinct from the stationary green pheromones.
Green & Blue Pheromones
Dropped by creatures when they are near the active food zone. Green pheromones are dropped near the green node during the day; blue ones near the blue node at night. They stay roughly where dropped, slowly fade, and attract other creatures toward that area. The Pheromone Strength slider directly controls how strong this attraction is. They help create group cohesion and guide creatures to the correct zone.
Red Hazard Particles
Passive threats that follow the vortex flow. On contact they cause direct energy damage + area-of-effect (AOE) damage to nearby creatures + a purple damage-over-time (DoT) flash. High-Sensor creatures take reduced damage and gain adaptation. When a red hazard reaches the center drain, it explodes and fires 4 red beams across the map that cause line damage.
Purple Damage-Over-Time (DoT) Flash
Visual + mechanical effect on creatures hit by red hazards. Causes ongoing energy drain for several seconds. If a creature's energy drops low enough during this, it can turn into a predator. High Sensor count reduces both initial damage and the DoT effect. Creatures gradually adapt to hazards over their lifetime.
Static Food Patches (Frozen Honey)
Created when yellow honey blooms time out due to vortex interference before reaching the main node. These act as secondary food sources — they slowly shrink, emit attraction pheromones, and creatures can eat from them directly.
