Future Vision
Ecosystem Architecture (Wall-Based Spatial Design)
Core Philosophy
Shift from walls being primarily defensive or filtering tools into **active instruments for spatial ecosystem design**. Players become "ecosystem architects" who use geometry, filters, and placement to deliberately shape creature behavior, population distribution, and resource flows across the map.
Key Gameplay Loop
• Observation: Watch how different creature types and particles move and interact.
• Intervention: Place angled filter walls to redirect, separate, or concentrate specific groups.
• Zone Creation: Carve out dedicated ecological niches (High-Module Cultivation Zones, Predator Containment, White Particle Farms, Breeding Sanctuaries, etc.).
• Dynamic Management: Continuously adjust walls as the ecosystem evolves — herding overflow populations, protecting vulnerable groups, or forcing interactions.
Powerful Patterns That Emerge
• "Shooting" clusters of a specific creature type into a target area using angled walls.
• Creating sorting systems where different module-count creatures are automatically separated.
• Building "particle pipelines" that guide white or red particles into collection zones.
• Establishing predator-prey balance through spatial barriers rather than direct combat.
• Long-term "ecosystem maintenance" gameplay where the player manages multiple interacting zones.
Supporting Features (Future)
• Movable / Draggable Walls — Reposition existing walls to fine-tune flows without rebuilding.
• Flow Strength & Direction — Control how aggressively a wall pushes (gentle guidance vs powerful herding).
• Directional / One-Way Filters — Walls that only allow passage or push in one direction.
• Wall Templates & Presets — Quick-drop patterns like "Sorting Gate", "Collection Funnel", "Containment Ring", "Migration Corridor".
• Visual Flow Indicators — Subtle arrows, particle trails, or glowing paths showing what a wall is currently influencing.
• Zone Markers — Optional visual overlays defining "intended" ecological regions.
Long-term Vision
The simulation becomes a living sandbox where the primary creative act is spatial composition. Players design and maintain complex, self-sustaining (or intentionally unstable) micro-ecosystems using walls as the main sculpting tool. This direction turns the game into something between a simulation toy, a spatial puzzle game, and an ecosystem management experience.
Future Vision
Spaceship Perimeter Mode + Strategic Bullet Deflection
Spaceship Perimeter Hug Mode
Add a new spaceship behavior/mode where the ship can "hug" the outer perimeter of the playable area and travel in a continuous circle while shooting inward toward the center. This creates a fundamentally different playstyle focused on:

• Area denial from the outside
• Circling support fire
• Herding creatures inward or preventing escape
• Creating dynamic "rotating pressure" on the ecosystem

The ship would feel like a mobile turret orbiting the entire map rather than a free-roaming fighter.
Strategic Bullet Deflection off Energy Walls
Make spaceship bullets (and potentially other projectiles) physically interact with all Energy Walls in interesting ways:

• Bullets bounce and ricochet off walls with proper angle-based reflection.
• Walls can be used tactically to redirect fire, create crossfire, or funnel bullets into specific zones.
• Different wall types could influence bullets differently (e.g. a strong "Red Particle" wall might absorb or weaken red-tinted bullets, while a solid barrier reflects cleanly).
• This turns the entire wall system into a layer of the combat/spatial puzzle — not just for creatures and particles, but for the player's own firepower as well.

This feature would make wall placement much more strategic during active spaceship use and deepen the "ecosystem architecture" fantasy by letting players design kill zones or safe corridors using geometry.
Major System
Energy Wall System – Full Specification
Important Rule
Every Selective Filter Wall must only affect its chosen object type and be completely transparent to everything else.

This means:
• A Predators filter wall only pushes actual predators. All other creatures (and particles) pass through unaffected.
• A Top 1% Highest Modules filter wall only pushes creatures with very high module counts. Everything else passes through.
• A Bottom 1% Lowest Modules filter wall only pushes low-module creatures. Everything else passes through.
• A White Particles filter wall only pushes actual floating white particles. All creatures and other particles pass through.
• A Red Particles filter wall only pushes actual floating red particles. All creatures and other particles pass through.

Core Principle: No filter wall should ever push or affect objects it wasn’t specifically told to target. This makes the system predictable, intentional, and much more useful for ecosystem design and herding.
1. Core Philosophy
Energy Walls are active spatial instruments. Their primary purpose is to let the player deliberately shape movement, separation, and interaction in the simulation.

They support two main playstyles:
• Architectural / Ecosystem Design — Using filters to sort, herd, protect, or concentrate specific groups.
• Offensive / Tactical — Using walls as damaging tools that can break, absorb resources (red particles), and convert creatures.
2. Placement & Interaction
Main way to create walls: Click + Drag.

• While dragging, a live preview line is shown.
• Creatures and particles near the preview line experience strong, immediate deflection (this gives the satisfying “sweeping through a crowd” feeling).
• When the mouse is released, the wall is committed and becomes a real object.
• Walls can be drawn at any angle and any length.

Other interactions:
• Right-click on an existing wall → Delete it.
• “Clear All Walls” button available in the placement menu.
3. Wall Types
When the player clicks the Energy Wall button, they get a clean menu with two main choices:

Solid Barrier
Pushes almost everything. Classic physical wall. Recommended for general blocking and containment.

Selective Filter Wall
Only affects the one chosen type. Everything else passes through normally. Recommended for precision herding, sorting, and protection.

After choosing Selective Filter Wall, the player picks exactly one filter type from this list:
• Predators
• White Particles
• Red Particles
• Top 1% Highest Module Creatures
• Bottom 1% Lowest Module Creatures
4. Offensive Mode (Toggle)
Available as a toggle in the placement menu.

When Offensive Mode is ON:
• The wall deals damage over time to anything it successfully pushes.
• Affected creatures get the purple damage effect and must eat white particles or risk becoming predators.
• The wall can absorb red particles.
• The wall is allowed to break/split when damaged.
• The wall can become empowered after absorbing enough red particles.

When Offensive Mode is OFF (default for most walls):
• The wall only pushes. No damage.
• It degrades extremely slowly.
• It does not react to red particles.
• It cannot break or become empowered.

This toggle lets the player decide whether a wall is a peaceful tool or a weapon.
5. Breaking & Splitting Behavior (Offensive Walls Only)
This is one of the most important mechanical features:

• Offensive walls lose life when creatures hit them repeatedly or when they absorb red particles.
• When life drops below thresholds, the wall breaks cleanly in half.
• After breaking, the new pieces gain temporary immunity (roughly 1.5–2 seconds) so they don’t chain-break instantly.
• This breaking process can happen multiple times.
• Larger walls can break more times than small ones.
• Very small pieces eventually disappear.

The goal is satisfying, readable destruction rather than chaotic fragmentation.
6. Red Particle Absorption & Empowerment (Offensive Only)
• Red particles that collide with an offensive wall are absorbed.
• Each absorption causes visual feedback (shake + energy pulse).
• After 5 absorptions, the wall becomes Empowered.
• Empowered walls gain these traits:
  – Much slower movement in the vortex.
  – They turn creatures into predators on contact.
  – Stronger visual appearance (color shift + more intense glow).

This creates a nice risk/reward loop: the player can “feed” their offensive walls with red particles to make them more dangerous.
7. Vortex Interaction
• By default, walls are vortex-immune. They stay where the player placed them.
• After taking significant damage (especially when they break), they become temporarily vulnerable to vortex forces for a short period.
• After the vulnerability window ends, they lock back into being vortex-immune.

This prevents walls from randomly drifting unless the player actively damages them.
8. Visual & Audio Feedback
• Peaceful walls have a clean, stable energy look.
• Offensive walls have a more aggressive appearance (stronger glow, more volatile inner effect).
• Different filter types can have subtle color or icon differences.
• Clear hit flash + shake when a wall takes damage or absorbs red particles.
• Satisfying visual + audio feedback when a wall breaks in half.
• Empowered walls have a distinct, more intense visual state.
9. Summary of Behavior Rules
Every Selective Filter Wall must only affect its chosen object type and be completely transparent to everything else. This is the core rule that governs all filter behavior.
Super Long-term Vision
Mobile Train System
A moving "train" entity that travels along a looping path around or through the map. The train could carry supplies/resources between zones and be equipped with a cannon for defense or area control.

This is a very high-level, ambitious future idea that would add a dynamic, moving element to the ecosystem architecture fantasy — a literal moving piece of infrastructure that players could potentially interact with, protect, or sabotage.
