Future Vision
Ecosystem Architecture (Wall-Based Spatial Design)
Core Philosophy
Shift from walls being primarily defensive or filtering tools into **active instruments for spatial ecosystem design**. Players become "ecosystem architects" who use geometry, filters, and placement to deliberately shape creature behavior, population distribution, and resource flows across the map.
Key Gameplay Loop
• Observation: Watch how different creature types and particles move and interact.
• Intervention: Place angled filter walls to redirect, separate, or concentrate specific groups.
• Zone Creation: Carve out dedicated ecological niches (High-Module Cultivation Zones, Predator Containment, White Particle Farms, Breeding Sanctuaries, etc.).
• Dynamic Management: Continuously adjust walls as the ecosystem evolves — herding overflow populations, protecting vulnerable groups, or forcing interactions.
Powerful Patterns That Emerge
• "Shooting" clusters of a specific creature type into a target area using angled walls.
• Creating sorting systems where different module-count creatures are automatically separated.
• Building "particle pipelines" that guide white or red particles into collection zones.
• Establishing predator-prey balance through spatial barriers rather than direct combat.
• Long-term "ecosystem maintenance" gameplay where the player manages multiple interacting zones.
Supporting Features (Future)
• Movable / Draggable Walls — Reposition existing walls to fine-tune flows without rebuilding.
• Flow Strength & Direction — Control how aggressively a wall pushes (gentle guidance vs powerful herding).
• Directional / One-Way Filters — Walls that only allow passage or push in one direction.
• Wall Templates & Presets — Quick-drop patterns like "Sorting Gate", "Collection Funnel", "Containment Ring", "Migration Corridor".
• Visual Flow Indicators — Subtle arrows, particle trails, or glowing paths showing what a wall is currently influencing.
• Zone Markers — Optional visual overlays defining "intended" ecological regions.
Long-term Vision
The simulation becomes a living sandbox where the primary creative act is spatial composition. Players design and maintain complex, self-sustaining (or intentionally unstable) micro-ecosystems using walls as the main sculpting tool. This direction turns the game into something between a simulation toy, a spatial puzzle game, and an ecosystem management experience.
Future Vision
Spaceship Perimeter Mode + Strategic Bullet Deflection
Spaceship Perimeter Hug Mode
Add a new spaceship behavior/mode where the ship can "hug" the outer perimeter of the playable area and travel in a continuous circle while shooting inward toward the center. This creates a fundamentally different playstyle focused on:

• Area denial from the outside
• Circling support fire
• Herding creatures inward or preventing escape
• Creating dynamic "rotating pressure" on the ecosystem

The ship would feel like a mobile turret orbiting the entire map rather than a free-roaming fighter.
Strategic Bullet Deflection off Energy Walls
Make spaceship bullets (and potentially other projectiles) physically interact with all Energy Walls in interesting ways:

• Bullets bounce and ricochet off walls with proper angle-based reflection.
• Walls can be used tactically to redirect fire, create crossfire, or funnel bullets into specific zones.
• Different wall types could influence bullets differently (e.g. a strong "Red Particle" wall might absorb or weaken red-tinted bullets, while a solid barrier reflects cleanly).
• This turns the entire wall system into a layer of the combat/spatial puzzle — not just for creatures and particles, but for the player's own firepower as well.

This feature would make wall placement much more strategic during active spaceship use and deepen the "ecosystem architecture" fantasy by letting players design kill zones or safe corridors using geometry.
Super Long-term Vision
Mobile Train System
A moving "train" entity that travels along a looping path around or through the map. The train could carry supplies/resources between zones and be equipped with a cannon for defense or area control.

This is a very high-level, ambitious future idea that would add a dynamic, moving element to the ecosystem architecture fantasy — a literal moving piece of infrastructure that players could potentially interact with, protect, or sabotage.
