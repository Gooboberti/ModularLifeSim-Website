Overview

MiniSimmy Orbital Ship v2 is an agent-based artificial life simulation. You are a steward, not a puppeteer: creatures think, cluster, feed, reproduce, and defend themselves through emergent rules. Your influence comes from environmental tools, selective pressure, meta progression (Gene Vault, Incubator, Ammo Factory), and — in map fullscreen — direct control of individual creatures.

The deepest layer of the simulation is the lace (also called the resonance web or colony network): the web of social connections between creatures. Lines you see on the map are not decorative. They represent real physical and informational bonds whose strength is computed every frame from module geometry, core identity, distance, and chrono rivalry.

The Lace & Connection Lines

Every creature carries orbiting modules arranged at specific angles around a central core. When two creatures are close enough, the game checks whether their outward-facing modules "point at" each other. Matching module types that face one another contribute to an alignment score. High alignment produces visible bonds and unlocks colony mechanics.

There are two distinct visual line types, plus transient overlays for feeding, stress, and communication:

Visual Line Legend

Weak lace bond — thin, faint purple/gray straight line. Forming acquaintance; not yet a full resonance link.
Resonance link — curved bezier arc between creatures. Active colony bond; carries energy, signals, and burden.
Feed glow overlay — bright green-yellow arc flash when a white particle strikes the link. Energy distributed to both endpoints and nearby linked allies.
Stress / snap / tear — red-tinted arc, sometimes visibly broken in the middle. Lace under tension, predator attack, or manual tug damage.
Repair glow — mint-green arc. Creatures are seaming a tear scar back together (costs energy).
Predator attack flash — deep red arc pulse when a hunter strikes a networked prey; triggers lace defense.
Traveling pulse dot — small bright point sliding along a link. This is colony communication or link activity propagating visually. Color matches the signal core (harvester = green, sensor = blue, etc.). Red dots indicate predator-stress signals.

Module Alignment — How Lines Form

Alignment is the mathematical heart of the lace. For each pair of creatures A and B:

  1. Distance must be between CREATURE_MIN_SEP (18px) and CREATURE_SOCIAL_MAX (52px). Too close or too far = zero alignment.
  2. Each non-core module on A whose outward angle faces toward B (within ~64°) is paired with each non-core module on B facing toward A.
  3. Matching module types add 1 + (tierA + tierB) × 0.12 per matching pair. Higher-tier modules lock together more strongly.
  4. Identical cores (both Harvester, both Sensor, etc. — not Balanced) add a flat +1.8 bonus.
  5. Chrono rivals (e.g. day-phase Harvester vs night-phase Sensor) multiply the score by 0.72.
  6. Final score is scaled by distance: score × (1 − distance/120).

The Inspector shows modules as colored dots orbiting the creature preview. Their layout angle is what matters for alignment — not just having the module, but having it face the neighbor. This is why colonies "look organized": creatures settle at ideal spacing (~23px) with modules interlocking.

Weak Bonds

When alignment is below 1.2 but above zero, a separate weak alignment score accumulates into laceWeakBond (max 1.35). Weak bonds:

  • Render as faint straight lines (not bezier curves).
  • Build faster when cores match (+45% rate).
  • Decay when strong resonance exists, or when weak alignment drops below 0.035.
  • Help manual control: dragging a controlled creature magnetizes weakly bonded allies, physically pulling them along.
  • Do not participate in full colony signal propagation (requires resonance links).

Resonance Links (Strong Bonds)

A resonance link requires all of:

  • Alignment ≥ 1.2
  • Distance between 18px and 52px
  • At least one creature has symbiosisLevel ≥ 1 (3+ aligned neighbors in cluster)
  • No active lace tear hole between the pair

Resonance links are drawn as animated quadratic bezier curves with a subtle swaying control point. Their color tint reflects the most recent colony signal core (Communicator echo turns links lavender, Sensor danger turns them blue, etc.).

Through resonance links, the simulation runs:

  • Energy equalization — energy flows from high to low across links, modulated by Communicator tiers and damped by Resistant tiers.
  • Network burden sharing — when a linked creature takes a big energy hit, ~42% of the loss propagates outward up to 8 hops, stressing the whole web.
  • Network size — each creature tracks how many others are reachable through resonance links (_networkSize). Many colony bonuses scale with this.
  • Predator paralysis — hunters within sensor range slow networked prey; Communicator-linked prey resist better.
  • Lace defense — when a predator damages a linked prey, allies donate energy and push the hunter back.

Particles Passing Along Links (Feed & Relay)

White energy particles are the most important link interaction. Two paths exist:

Direct link feed

When a white particle passes near a resonance link's bezier curve (not necessarily near a creature body), feedResonanceLinkHit fires:

  • Both endpoint creatures receive ~44% of the spread energy each.
  • Any creature within 34px of the hit point with alignment ≥ 1.0 to either endpoint receives ~20%.
  • The link gains feedGlow and pullBoost — visibly brighter arc and stronger social pull.
  • Both endpoints get _colonyLinkPulse — the traveling dot animation along the link.
  • Creatures think: "Web caught a drift particle".
  • Colony Feast Relay tech and _relayBoost on the particle amplify all of the above.

Saturated creature relay

Creatures above 75% energy (WHITE_SATURATED_RELAY_THRESHOLD) do not absorb whites. They relay them into the lace — redirecting particle velocity toward hungry allies, nearest link feed points, or the colony deep target. See the White Particle Relay section.

The traveling pulse dots you see on links are the visual echo of _colonyLinkPulse, feedGlow, and signal propagation — the lace "lighting up" as information and energy move through the web.

Colony Communication

Beyond passive energy flow, networked creatures actively emit thought signals to their resonance-linked neighbors. This is the simulation's communication layer — the "particles passing back and forth" that carry meaning, not just calories.

Thought Signals by Core Type

Each frame, a rotating batch of creatures with symbiosisLevel ≥ 1 runs computeColonyEmit. If conditions are met, they broadcast a directional signal:

Harvester Signal: feast

Emits toward green food zone when hungry or when food is abundant; toward food blooms within 95px. Thoughts: "Hive hungers…", "Bloom ripples through the lace", "Sun-ripe food ripples through the lace".

Sensor Signal: danger

Emits away from nearest hazard; or toward blue zone at night. Thoughts: "Unease travels the colony", "Danger echoed down the line!". Receivers gain _colonyHazardBoost.

Mover Signal: rush

When moving fast, broadcasts velocity direction. Thought: "A rush ripples outward". Helps colony flow and slipstream relay behavior.

Resistant Signal: hold

In clusters of 3+, emits toward cluster centroid. Thought: "The colony holds steady". Reinforces defensive folding.

Explorer Signal: wonder

At map edges or near harvestable module particles. Thoughts: "Frontier whispers at the edge", "A find travels the network". Receivers gain _colonyExploreBoost.

Communicator Signal: echo

Amplifies the strongest neighbor tug; or hums when symbiosis count ≥ 3. Thoughts: "The hive speaks as one", "Threads hum in agreement". Can relay signals two hops through the network.

Balanced Signal: murmur

Weak hazard retreat or food-zone lean. Thought: "A faint murmur in the weave".

Signal propagation rules

  • Signals travel only across resonance links (alignment ≥ 1.2).
  • Reception strength = signal.strength × (0.32 + alignment×0.11 + communicatorTiers×0.09 + symbiosis×0.05), damped 22% for Resistant cores.
  • Communicator cores (or any creature with 2+ Communicator modules) relay signals to neighbors-of-neighbors at ~46–56% strength — this is how danger at the colony edge reaches the interior.
  • Signals decay: tug vectors ×0.9 and strength ×0.93 per frame; hazard/food/explore boosts decay ×0.94.

Colony Tug & Link Pulses

When a creature receives a signal, it accumulates _colonyTug — a directional urge (vx, vy, strength) plus an optional thought string. If symbiosis ≥ 1 or Communicator tiers ≥ 1, applyColonyTug converts this into real velocity changes.

Visual feedback chain:

  1. Emitter thinks a colony thought (shown in Inspector).
  2. Receiver gets _colonyLinkPulse = 20 and _colonyLinkSignalCore set to emitter's core color.
  3. Resonance link between them brightens and shows a traveling pulse dot in that core's color.
  4. Receiver physically drifts in the signal direction (food pull, hazard flee, etc.).

Edge-aware tug: near map edges, colony tug is blended toward map center to prevent corner ejection — a recent stability fix for dense colonies.

White Particle Relay System

White particles are floating energy orbs from the vortex and death releases. They are the colony's shared calorie pool. The relay system prevents wasted energy when individuals are full.

Absorption vs Relay

  • Below 75% energy: creature absorbs the particle — energy fills, overflow becomes honey (Harvester bonus), fullness knockback may eject the creature, and reproduction urge may trigger.
  • At or above 75% energy: creature relays — particle is redirected, not eaten. Flash text: RELAY. Thought varies by core (see below).
  • mustEatWhite (purple infection): infected creatures cannot relay; they must absorb whites to cleanse.

Per-Core Relay Behavior

Harvester "Bloom surplus — diving to the feast heart" — pulls toward colony center; may spill honey as a food bloom if honey > 0.8.

Sensor "Oracle ping — the web will catch it" — aims particle at nearest link feed point; pings neighbors with food boost.

Mover "Slipstream pass" — high redirect velocity with tangential slipstream push along link direction.

Resistant "Bulwark channel" — anchors deeper in lace; shares food boost with aligned allies.

Explorer "Frontier gift" — marks gift trail toward hungry allies (lower fullness score).

Communicator "Hive relay" — magnetizes particle to resonance links; boosts pullBoost and feedGlow on all personal links.

Archetype overlays (Sunweaver, Gloam Choir, Bloomkeeper, Hive Sovereign, etc.) further modify relay pull, link seek, and honey spill. Skill tree perks at tier 8 and legendaries stack on top.

Colony Feast Relay (tech tree, up to 5 levels): global upgrade widening link catch radius and strengthening colony dive when relaying. Parent node: Module Resonance.

On relay, the creature sets _colonyDeepUrge for 110 frames (~1.8s at 60fps), steering toward findColonyDeepTarget — the weighted center of its aligned cluster, blended toward map center near edges.

_networkSize counts all creatures reachable through resonance links (graph traversal). Colony defense, predator paralysis resistance, and triumph rewards all reference this number. A creature alone has network size 0–1; a thriving colony may reach dozens.

Lace Stress, Tears & Repair

The lace is elastic but not unbreakable. Several forces stress it:

  • Compression — creatures pushed closer than ideal distance (~23px) store compression debt. When they expand, both pay energy (LACE_COMPRESS_STRETCH_COST).
  • Manual tug — dragging a controlled creature stretches the lace anchor. Allies beyond LACE_ELASTIC_IDEAL (30px) accumulate tension; cutting links at hop-0 can snap them.
  • Predator attacks — damage propagates as network burden; links flash red.
  • Overlap crowding — dissimilar creatures overlapping drain energy; similar cores drain less.

Snap & Tear

When link tension exceeds LACE_TEAR_SNAP_TENSION (0.58) on a cut link (creature pulled away from anchor), the link can snap:

  • Visual gap appears in the arc (two separated red line segments).
  • laceTorn and laceSnapped timers activate.
  • Tear scar recorded for 150 frames; first 38 frames = "hole" (no link possible even if alignment recovers).
  • 11% chance of death on severe tears (LACE_TEAR_DEATH_CHANCE).
  • After hole phase, creatures can heal by closing to ideal distance at alignment ≥ 1.12 — mint repair glow, energy seam cost.

After manual release, applyLaceSnapbackRipple sucks the colony back toward the anchor with a burden wave — creatures think "Snapping back into the lace…" then "Settling back with the colony…"

Predators & Colony Defense

How Creatures Become Predators

  • Starvation pressure — low energy + neural predator tendency output.
  • Social pressure — 5+ creature clusters where your specialization is rare (orange Inspector background).
  • Purple infection — Prune mode spores; must eat white or flip predator (purple background).
  • Lace shock — network burden from ally deaths can flip low-energy creatures: "Lace shock — turned hunter".
  • Player feast mode — manual toggle (fullscreen key 1).

Predator Link Paralysis

Within sensor detect range (~52px base, extended by Sensor tiers), predators slow prey. Mover, Resistant, Communicator, Explorer, and Sensor modules each reduce paralysis. Communicator-linked prey in large networks resist best. At extreme slowdown near contact, prey can freeze.

Colony Pressure Defense

When a networked prey is hunted, aligned allies within overlap range become defenders. They fold inward (pressure fold green ring), stacking damage on the predator. Two or more defenders:

  • Damage scales with defender count squared.
  • Predator slowed and thinks "The colony closes in…" or "Crushed by the lace!"
  • On kill: Lace triumph — energy bonus, lace valor, reproduction urge, food bloom, score award to all aligned allies.

Modules, Cores & Archetypes

Six module types orbit each creature. Three of the same type and tier merge into the next tier (I–X). The dominant module type becomes the core (requires 3+ tier count). The core sets chrono affinity, colony signal type, white relay profile, and neural brain bias.

Harvester

Pro: food intake. Con: lower max energy (−3.2 per tier). Day/green affinity.

Sensor

Pro: hazard awareness. Con: speed penalty. Night/blue affinity. Rival of Harvester.

Mover

Pro: speed, vortex escape. Con: movement energy drain. Night lean.

Resistant

Pro: damage reduction, lifespan. Con: slow. Neutral chrono; dampens network burden.

Explorer

Pro: exploration range, death echoes. Con: weaker social alignment. Flex chrono.

Communicator

Pro: social align, signal relay. Con: combat weakness. Flex chrono; amplifies lace defense 25% (legendary).

Archetypes (hybrid identities, tier band ≥ 4)

Sunweaver, Gloam Choir, Bloomkeeper, Storm Warden, Vortex Runner, Hive Sovereign, Predator Apex (3+ kills), Echo Founder (prestige). Each requires specific module tier counts and grants chrono locks, brain biases, and relay overrides.

Tier Bands

Band 0: starter. Band 1: peak tier 2+ or 3+ modules. Band 2: peak 4+ or 6+ total. Band 3: peak 7+ or 14+ total. Higher bands add social alignment and reproduction bias to the brain.

Day/Night & Chrono Affinity

The world alternates day and night. Food zones shift:

  • Green zone — active herbivore feeding during day.
  • Blue zone — active feeding during night (Sensor-favored).
  • Chrono-in-phase creatures gain +6% eat multiplier and stronger zone pull.
  • Harvester vs Sensor are chrono rivals — they physically repel when in opposing phases, weakening alignment by 28%.

Flex-phase cores (Explorer, Communicator) dynamically lean green or blue based on zone richness, energy, and colony tug direction.

Neural Brain System

Every creature runs a 12 → 12 → 7 feed-forward neural net every other frame, combined with module identity biases.

12 Inputs

  • 0 — Energy (0–1)
  • 1 — Honey (0–1)
  • 2 — Is day? (1/0)
  • 3 — Hazard distance
  • 4–5 — Hazard angle sin/cos
  • 6 — Nearby creature density
  • 7–9 — Harvester/Sensor/Mover tier counts
  • 10 — Adaptation level (0–11)
  • 11 — Distance from map center

7 Outputs

  • 0 — Food drive
  • 1 — Vortex escape
  • 2 — Hazard avoidance
  • 3 — Social alignment
  • 4 — Exploration
  • 5 — Reproduction will
  • 6 — Predator tendency

Brains copy to offspring with mutation. The Inspector neural diagram shows connection strength — brighter green = reinforced through experience. Module cores add static biases (e.g. Harvester +food drive, Communicator +social align, −predator tend).

All Particle Types

Yellow honey — Deposited by well-fed creatures. Drifts toward food zones; becomes brown food blooms.

Creature Life & Reproduction

  • Energy — 0 to max (72–100; Harvester lowers cap). Death at 0.
  • Honey — secondary storage up to 26; spills as yellow particles / blooms.
  • Lifespan — Resistant modules extend max age; age ticks each frame.
  • Reproduction — neural repro will + particle repro urge + environment reproMult. Costs energy; copies/mutates brain and modules.
  • Newborn repro cooldown — 420 frames (~7s) after birth prevents chain-reproduction explosions.
  • Particle reproduction — absorbing a white at full energy triggers urge (110 frames); offspring spawns if cooldowns clear.
  • Traits — heritable: pheromone sensitivity, exploration bias, vortex resistance, hazard avoidance.

Manual Control & Feast Mode

In map fullscreen, select a creature and click Control in the Inspector (or use hotkeys). Drag to tug; the lace stretches elastically and weak-bonded allies follow.

Feast Mode (Key 1)

Toggles _manualSuperPredator. While active:

  • Creature becomes a predator hunting the weakest prey in social range (52px).
  • Primary target takes 1.65 damage/frame; splash victims take 0.42/frame (scaled by Mover tiers and time scale) — roughly ~1 second to kill at 60fps.
  • Red feast lines draw to all victims; pulsing dot on weakest target.
  • On kill: feast ends, energy restored to 100, offspring born at prey location inheriting 1–3 stolen modules, score awarded, auto-lineage handoff optional.
  • Press 1 again to cancel early.

Progression & Economy

High Roller Points — earned from survival, kills, colony triumphs, births, etc. Multiplied by population/predator/module/tier boosts (crystal-purchasable, doubling cost and power each level).

Crystals — run currency for boosts, tool rentals, slot unlocks. Dust — permanent prestige currency. Furnace — burns crystals for temporary score multiplier.

Extract → Incubate → Vault — extract creatures to eggs (Inspector or Top 10); Incubator grows them over cycles; Gene Vault persists across prestige. Slot occupancy multiplies score (×100, ×200, ×400…).

Bottom 10 / Ammo Factory — cull weak creatures for antimatter crafting; ship cannon fires module-typed damage.
