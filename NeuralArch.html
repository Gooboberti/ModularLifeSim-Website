01
The Lower Brain — Individual Neural Nets

Each creature possesses a sparse 12 → 12 → 7 neural network. At birth, brains are initialized sparsely (~27% density) and inherit a mutated copy of their parent’s weights through the reproduce() method.

Generational Mutation
On reproduction: brain.copy() → mutate(0.085, 0.36)
Lifetime Hebbian Learning
Every forward pass updates coactivity and applies Hebbian weight changes when pre- and post-synaptic activity correlate during successful behavior.

02
Embodied Module Influence

Modules are not just passive bonuses. They actively shape the lower brain through pathway-specific bias and spatial layout mapping.

Two simultaneous effects:
  1. Direct statistical modifiers (food gain, hazard detection, social alignment, etc.)
  2. Structural rewiring of neural pathways via applyEmbodiedStructure()
MODULE → BRAIN MAPPING
Harvester → food & bloom pathways
Sensor → hazard & oracle pathways
Mover → speed & slipstream pathways
Resistant → burden & soak pathways
Explorer → frontier & echo pathways
Communicator → lace & resonance pathways
03
The Lace as Distributed Higher Brain

The lace network functions as a collective external brain. Through lace neuromodulation, colony-level signals retune individual learning rates, gains, cohesion, and lineage bias in real time.

NEUROMODULATION
Colony signals dynamically adjust plasticity, food/hazard sensitivity, and social cohesion across linked creatures.
SIGNAL PROPAGATION
Neural information and successful behavioral patterns spread through resonance links, creating shared memory across the macroorganism.
GROUP-LEVEL MEMORY
Lineage echoes traveling through the lace can inject ancestral synaptic patterns into living creatures when core types align.
04
Gene Vault & Vortex-Mediated Curation

High-value brain + module configurations can be preserved long-term through the Gene Vault. Special white particles (soul echoes) that survive the journey to the vortex center become candidates for vaulted spawning.
