Modular Life Sim
Roadmap

An accessible, educational browser-based artificial life simulation focused on modular agents, evolution, and emergent behavior.

THE VISION

Understanding through simulation

Modular Life Sim is an ongoing experiment to create a deep but accessible artificial life platform. It draws inspiration from the original Python Modular Life Sim while making the systems observable, playable, and educational in the browser.

The long-term goal is to build intuition around complex systems, evolution, emergence, and the relationship between individual agent behavior and large-scale ecosystem dynamics.

CORE PRINCIPLES

What guides development

Modular Agents

Creatures are composed of visual, functional modules rather than being monolithic entities.

Neural Decision Making

Agents use a consistent neural architecture (6 inputs) with recurrent memory for observable behavior.

Evolution Through Pressure

Meaningful selection pressure via energy costs, predation, and resource competition drives improvement over generations.

Inheritance + Mutation

Modules and neural traits are inherited with controlled mutation, enabling open-ended evolution.

Environmental Coupling

Day/night cycles and resource zones create rich feedback between agents and their environment.

Educational Clarity

Every major system should be observable. The simulation should teach as much as it entertains.

DEVELOPMENT ROADMAP

Phased approach

1

Phase 1: Stable Core

Current

Build a reliable, playable foundation that captures the essence of the original Python simulation.

Key Deliverables
  • • Yellow orb + module visualization
  • • Green/Blue environmental zones
  • • 6-input neural decision system
  • • Predation with inheritance
  • • Graphical neural net inspector
  • • Tractor beam mechanics
  • • Reproduction + mutation
  • • Energy bar with predatory threshold
2

Phase 2: Evolutionary Pressure

Introduce real selection pressure so evolution becomes the main driver of complexity.

Module Costs

Every module drains energy. More modules = higher risk during scarcity.

Mutation

Controlled chance to gain/lose modules and mutate neural memory on birth.

Resource Feedback

Local food depletion and slower regeneration when overpopulated.

3

Phase 3: Cognitive & Ecosystem Depth

Deepen agent intelligence and create conditions for richer long-term dynamics.

Neural Improvements
  • • Stronger recurrent memory
  • • Simple lifetime learning
  • • Better neighbor awareness
Ecosystem Dynamics
  • • Strong boom/crash cycles
  • • Environmental variability
  • • Co-evolution pressure
4

Phase 4: Maturity & Polish

Make the simulation beautiful, observable, and deeply educational.

  • • Excellent UI/UX and onboarding
  • • Lineage and family tree viewer
  • • Long-term statistics and trends
  • • Multiple parallel experiments
  • • Limited signaling system
  • • Strong educational explanations
