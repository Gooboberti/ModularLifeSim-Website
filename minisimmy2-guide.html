Getting Started

MiniSimmy2 is an agent-based artificial life simulation. You do not directly control creatures. Instead, you influence the ecosystem through tools, hazards, and long-term lineage management.

Your First 10 Minutes

  1. Watch how creatures move in the vortex and gather at the green/blue food zones.
  2. Click on any creature to open the Inspector and see its modules.
  3. Try placing an Energy Wall (click the button in the quick tools).
  4. Enable Red Hazards from the Cheats store to create selective pressure.
  5. Let the simulation run for a while and observe which creatures survive and reproduce.

Core Concepts

Emergence Over Scripting

Interesting behavior comes from simple rules interacting over time, not from hand-crafted creature scripts. The most surprising outcomes usually appear after 10–30 minutes of simulation time.

Modules Are the Brain

Creatures don’t have traditional AI. Their behavior is determined by the combination and tier of modules they carry. More modules = more complex behavior.

Long-term Progression

The real depth comes from the Gene Vault. You are not just playing one simulation — you are cultivating bloodlines across many resets.

Player as Steward

You influence the environment and apply selective pressure. The creatures evolve in response to the conditions you create.

Creatures & Modules

Every creature is an instance of the Creature class. They carry position, velocity, energy, a list of modules, generation number, and several personality traits.

Module Types

Module Color Primary Effect High Tier Benefit
Harvester Increases energy gained from food zones and white particles Much larger collection radius
Sensor Improves hazard detection and avoidance quality Predictive dodging from far away
Mover Helps escape the vortex center Extremely strong spiral escape
Resistant Reduces energy drain rate Excellent during Drought phases
Explorer Increases random movement and exploration Breaks movement loops and stuck behavior
Communicator Strengthens pheromone signals and hazard warnings Very effective group coordination

Tier Merging Rule

When a creature has 3 modules of the same type at the same tier, they automatically merge into one module of the next higher tier. This happens in checkForTierUpgrades().

How Creatures Behave

Creature behavior emerges from the combination of their modules, current energy, environment state, and proximity to other creatures and hazards.

Behavior Priority (Rough Order)

  1. Hazard Avoidance — Strongest immediate reaction if Sensors detect danger.
  2. Energy Seeking — Move toward the currently active food zone.
  3. Social Alignment — Match velocity with similar neighbors (flocking by module type).
  4. Vortex Forces — Constant rotational pull from the center.
  5. Reproduction — When energy is high enough and cooldown is ready.
  6. Predator Behavior — Only active when isPredator is true.

Predator Conversion

Creatures can turn into predators when energy is very low or when social pressure in large groups becomes too high. This is an emergent survival strategy.

Honey & Blooms

Creatures with excess energy create honey. When they deposit it, it slowly moves toward food zones and becomes a new food patch when it stops.

Environment & Cycles

Fertile
High energy gain • High reproduction rate • Easier survival
Normal
Balanced values. Standard difficulty.
Drought
High energy drain • Low reproduction • Rewards Resistant and Explorer creatures.

Hazards & Player Tools

Red Hazard Particles

Basic damaging particles. Strong selective pressure that heavily rewards Sensor modules. Can be toggled and intensity-adjusted in the Cheats store.

Cursor Hazard

Turns your mouse into a damaging field with a pheromone aura. Also absorbs nearby red particles. Extremely powerful for direct intervention and clearing space.

Energy Walls (v1.95+)

Player-placed barriers with two main modes:

  • Solid — Affects all creatures. Good for general containment.
  • Selective Filter — Only affects specific types (Predators, High Module count, Low Module count, or specific particles). Unaffected creatures pass straight through.

Progression & Economy

Progression happens on two timescales:

  • Short-term: High Roller Points earned from survival, clustering, and kills. Spent in the Cheats store and converted into Aetherium Dust via Prestige.
  • Long-term: Aetherium Crystals and the Gene Vault. This is where real power accumulates across multiple simulations.

The Furnace

Burning Aetherium Dust in the Furnace gives a temporary point multiplier (up to x100). The longer you burn, the higher the multiplier becomes.

Gene Vault System

The Gene Vault is the most important long-term system in MiniSimmy2. It lets you preserve high-quality creature brains across simulation resets.

How it works:
• Extract strong creatures into Eggs
• Incubate eggs over time
• Store finished eggs in the Gene Vault
• During prestige, high-quality brains in the vault influence future generations through special prestige particles

Creature Inspector

Click any creature to open the Inspector. It shows energy, modules, generation, kills, children, current thought, and a live visualization of the creature’s internal state. This is your main tool for understanding what is actually happening in the simulation.

Music Modes

Prune Mode
Aggressive • Strong vortex • Heavy red hazards • Inverted food timing. Good for culling weak creatures.
Replicate Mode
Growth-focused • Large food fields • High reproduction • No hazards. Good for population booms.
Evolve Mode
Mutation-focused • Special modules spawn • Changing vortex direction. Good for experimentation.

Tips & Strategies

Early Game: Focus on building a stable population. Don’t go too hard on hazards until you have some Sensor creatures.
Mid Game: Start applying selective pressure. Use Energy Walls and Red Hazards to reward good module combinations.
Late Game: Use the Gene Vault aggressively. Extract your best creatures before prestiging so you can carry quality forward.
Pro Tip: Large groups of the same specialization can create internal social pressure that turns some creatures into predators. This is usually bad unless you want predators.

Controls Reference

General

  • Click creature — Open Inspector
  • Space — Pause / Unpause
  • + / - — Change simulation speed
  • Mouse wheel — Zoom in/out

Quick Tools

  • Hazard Fields — Toggle red particles
  • Cursor Hazard — Turn mouse into damage tool
  • Energy Wall — Draw barriers
  • Spaceship — Toggle controllable ship
