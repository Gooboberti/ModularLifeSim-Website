Getting Started

MiniSimmy2 is an agent-based artificial life simulation. You do not directly control creatures. Instead, you influence the ecosystem through tools, hazards, selective pressure, and long-term lineage management via the Gene Vault.

Your First 10 Minutes

  1. Watch how creatures move in the vortex and gather at the green/blue food zones.
  2. Click on any creature to open the Inspector — watch its modules, current thought, and live neural outputs.
  3. Try placing an Energy Wall using the quick tools.
  4. Enable Red Hazards from the Cheats store to create selective pressure.
  5. Observe which creatures survive, reproduce, and pass on better brains over time.

Core Concepts

Emergence Over Scripting

Interesting behavior comes from simple rules interacting over time. The most surprising outcomes usually appear after 10–40 minutes of simulation.

Modules + Neural Brain

Creatures don’t have traditional AI. Their behavior is determined by the combination of modules they carry + a small neural network brain that makes real-time decisions.

Long-term Progression

The real depth comes from the Gene Vault. You are cultivating bloodlines across multiple simulation resets. Brains are copied and mutated when creatures reproduce.

Player as Steward

You influence the environment and apply selective pressure. Creatures evolve in response to the conditions you create — especially through hazards, walls, and lifespan mechanics.

Neural Brain System

NEW

Every creature has a small neural network (NeuralNet) that makes real-time decisions. The brain is copied to offspring and mutated slightly during reproduction. This is the core of creature “personality” and decision making.

Brain Inputs (12 values)

  • 0. Energy level (normalized 0–1)
  • 1. Honey level (normalized 0–1)
  • 2. Is it currently Day? (1 or 0)
  • 3. Normalized distance to nearest hazard
  • 4. sin() of hazard angle
  • 5. cos() of hazard angle
  • 6. Number of nearby creatures (normalized)
  • 7. Tiered Harvester module count
  • 8. Tiered Sensor module count
  • 9. Tiered Mover module count
  • 10. Adaptation level (0–11)
  • 11. Distance to vortex center (normalized)

Brain Outputs (7 decision values)

  • [0] Food Drive — How strongly it wants to go to the active food zone
  • [1] Vortex Escape — How strongly it tries to escape the vortex center
  • [2] Hazard Avoidance — How strongly it reacts to detected hazards
  • [3] Social Alignment — How strongly it matches velocity with similar neighbors
  • [4] Exploration — Amount of random movement / breaking of patterns
  • [5] Reproduction Will — Influences when it feels ready to reproduce
  • [6] Predator Tendency — How likely it is to turn into a predator under social pressure

The brain runs every other frame. Its outputs are used to modulate almost every major behavior: hazard dodging strength, food zone attraction, vortex escape force, social cohesion, exploration randomness, and the chance of becoming a predator when under social pressure in large groups.

When a creature reproduces, its brain is copied and then mutated (weights are slightly randomized). This creates heritable “personalities” across generations.

Creatures & Modules

Every creature is an instance of the Creature class. They carry position, velocity, energy, modules, generation, personality traits, a neural brain, and now a dynamic lifespan.

Module Types

Module Color Primary Effect High Tier / Special Benefit
Harvester Increases energy gain from food zones & white particles Much larger collection radius + bloom creation
Sensor Improves hazard detection quality and avoidance Predictive dodging + better adaptation gain
Mover Helps escape the vortex center Extremely strong spiral escape forces
Resistant Reduces energy drain rate +0.25 seconds lifespan per tier level + excellent in Drought
Explorer Increases random movement and exploration Breaks movement loops + strong vortex escape
Communicator Strengthens pheromone signals and hazard warnings Very effective group coordination + bloom creation

Tier Merging Rule

When a creature has 3 modules of the same type at the same tier, they automatically merge into one module of the next higher tier during the periodic upgrade check. This is how creatures become “elite” specialists over generations.

Specialization Roles

Creatures with high tier counts in one module type gain special role titles (Master Harvester, Hazard Warden, Vortex Runner, Survivor Core, Wanderer Elite, Signal Leader). These are mostly cosmetic but indicate strong specialization.

Lifespan & Age System

NEW

Creatures now have a dynamic lifespan system that makes higher-generation creatures more valuable and rewards investment in the Resistant module.

Generation-Based Lifespan

  • Base (Gen 1): 15 seconds
  • +3 seconds per generation above 1
  • Light randomness is still applied (± ~2 seconds)

Example: A Gen 5 creature has a base lifespan of ~27 seconds before any bonuses.

Resistant Module Lifespan Bonus

Every tier level of Resistant modules a creature carries adds +0.25 seconds to its maximum lifespan.

Example: A creature with a Tier 2 Resistant module (counts as 2) + one Tier 1 Resistant = 3 total tier levels → +0.75 seconds lifespan.

Aging Effects

  • Creatures begin to visually grey after ~30–40% of their lifespan.
  • Movement speed gradually slows down as they age (age slowdown).
  • When age exceeds maxAge, the creature dies immediately and is removed from the world.
  • Death is handled cleanly (deselects if it was the inspected creature).

Lineage & Reproduction

Reproduction is the core of long-term progression. When a creature reproduces:

  • It passes a copy of its current modules to the child.
  • Its neural brain is copied and then mutated (weights are slightly randomized).
  • The child’s generation is set to parent generation + 1.
  • The child inherits slightly mutated versions of the parent’s four personality traits.
  • Higher generation creatures have significantly longer natural lifespans.

This system creates heritable “personalities” and module combinations. Good brains and good module setups can persist and improve across many generations if the lineage survives.

How Creatures Behave

Creature behavior emerges from the combination of modules, neural brain outputs, energy level, environment, social context, and age.

Behavior Priority (Rough Order)

  1. Hazard Avoidance — Strongly modulated by brain output [2] and Sensor modules.
  2. Energy Seeking — Modulated by brain output [0] (Food Drive) + hunger level.
  3. Vortex Escape — Modulated by brain output [1] + Mover/Explorer modules.
  4. Social Alignment & Cohesion — Modulated by brain output [3].
  5. Exploration / Randomness — Modulated by brain output [4].
  6. Reproduction — Influenced by brain output [5] + energy level.
  7. Predator Behavior — Triggered when energy is very low or by social pressure + brain output [6].

Predator Conversion

Creatures can turn into predators when energy is very low or when social pressure in large groups becomes too high (especially if their brain’s Predator Tendency output is high). Once they turn, they hunt weaker creatures for energy and modules.

Honey & Blooms

Creatures with high Harvester or Communicator modules and excess honey can deposit food blooms. This is both a survival strategy and a way to help the group.

Environment & Cycles

Fertile
High energy gain • High reproduction • Easier survival. Good for population growth and testing new lineages.
Normal
Balanced values. Standard difficulty. Most common state.
Drought
High energy drain • Low reproduction • Strongly rewards Resistant + Explorer creatures. Good for culling weak lineages.

Hazards & Player Tools

Red Hazard Particles

Damaging particles that create strong selective pressure. Rewards good Sensor modules and adaptation. Intensity is adjustable in the Cheats store.

Cursor Hazard

Turns your mouse into a damaging field. Also absorbs nearby red particles. Extremely powerful for direct selective pressure.

Energy Walls

Player-placed barriers. Can be Solid (affects everything) or Selective Filter (only affects specific types like Predators, High-module, or Low-module creatures). Can be made offensive.

Progression & Economy

Progression happens on two main timescales:

  • Short-term: High Roller Points earned during a run. Spent in the Cheats store or converted via Prestige.
  • Long-term: Aetherium Crystals + Gene Vault. This is where real power carries across resets. Good brains and module combinations are preserved here.

The Furnace

Burning Dust gives a temporary point multiplier (up to x100). Longer burns = significantly higher multiplier. Use this strategically before big prestige runs.

Gene Vault System

The Gene Vault is the most important long-term system. It lets you preserve high-quality creature brains across simulation resets.

How it works:
• Extract strong creatures into Eggs (captures their current brain + modules)
• Incubate eggs over time
• Store finished eggs in the Gene Vault
• During prestige, high-quality brains from the vault influence the starting population of the next run

Creature Inspector

Click any creature to open the Inspector. It shows energy, modules, generation, kills, children, current thought, age progress, and live neural brain outputs. The inspector now updates in real-time every frame while a creature is selected, giving you live insight into its decision making.

Music Modes

Prune Mode
Aggressive • Strong vortex • Heavy red hazards • Good for culling weak creatures and testing lineage robustness.
Replicate Mode
Growth-focused • Large food fields • High reproduction • Good for population booms and spreading good brains.
Evolve Mode
Mutation-focused • Special modules spawn more often • Changing vortex direction. Good for exploring new combinations.

Tips & Strategies

Early Game: Focus on building a stable population and letting some generations pass before applying heavy hazards. Let good brains start spreading.
Mid Game: Use Energy Walls and Red Hazards strategically to reward good module combinations (especially Sensor + Resistant). Watch the Inspector to see which brains are performing well.
Late Game: Aggressively use the Gene Vault. Extract your best creatures (high generation + strong brain + good modules) before prestiging. Resistant modules become very valuable for long lineages.

Controls Reference

General

  • Click creature — Open Inspector (real-time updates)
  • Space — Pause / Unpause
  • + / - — Change simulation speed (affects timeScale)

Quick Tools

  • Hazard Fields — Toggle red particles
  • Cursor Hazard — Turn mouse into damage tool
  • Energy Wall — Draw solid or selective barriers
